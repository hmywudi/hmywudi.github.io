Research
Performance Analysis of Quantum Variational Classifier under Adversarial Attacks
Mingyang Hao

This thesis explores how different architectures of QVCs affect their performance, efficiency, and robustness to adversarial attacks on the Fashion-MNIST dataset. It compares four QVC architectures: chain, circle, star, and all-to-all, and finds that the star topology is the most efficient and accurate, while the chain and all-to-all topologies are more resistant to adversarial attacks. It also shows that QVCs learn more robust and informative features than classical models, and that QVCs may have a hierarchical learning capability. The study contributes to the field of quantum machine learning by providing novel insights into the design and optimization of QVCs and propose a further research direction: How did QVC learn stronger feature than conventional NNs?
